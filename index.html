PHARAOH

Author: Zachary Christensen

Computers are amazing tools. The human brain is obviously more powerful, but without computers we wouldn't have figured out many of the things we know. The creative problem solving is done by the person, but a lot of times it would take a person very much time to employ the solution to get the answer or even more time than they would be alive. An example would be finding a very large prime number for whatever purpose you may need it.

Unknown to my knowledge there may be creative and intelligent ways for people to find a large prime number but barring those possibities, a computer would be neccessary for the task. To find a prime number this way, one would have to try each negative number to up to about half of half that number to see if it is divisible by anything. New and creative proccesses are best done within the domain of human intelligence but the raw power of being able to do almost innumerable calculations is exponentially faster and more efficient on a computer.

Computers compute and people solve. It is entirely possible to have a solution that is nearly or is impossible to employ. I run into this when trying to wrtie songs on guitar. I try to write things I can play by myself, but every once in awhile the note that would be the solution to resolve the tension built is out of my fingers reach. I know that is the solution, but I can not successfully employ it due to my human limitations. On a computer, however, any amount of any notes no matter the distance or overlap, can be played by a single machine.
